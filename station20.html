OEBC Mock OSCE – Station 20

Module 3 – Anterior Segment & Adnexa • Pterygium (Interactive)

Step 1 – Read the Station

Exam Context: On the real OEBC OSCE, you would have about 8 minutes at this station.

Scenario:
A 42-year-old outdoor construction worker presents for a routine eye exam. He reports a “yellow growth on the white of my eye” that has been slowly increasing in size over the last few years. It sometimes feels gritty or irritated, especially in sun and wind. Vision remains 20/20 OU.

Slit Lamp Findings:

  • Nasal triangular fibrovascular growth extending 2.0 mm onto the cornea OD
  • Mild conjunctival injection adjacent to lesion
  • No ulceration or dellen
  • OS with small pinguecula

Your task: Explain the diagnosis and management plan in clear, patient-friendly language, including:

  • What a pterygium is
  • Relation to UV/environmental exposure
  • Impact on comfort/vision
  • Non-surgical management
  • When surgery is recommended and recurrence risk
  • Follow-up plan

Step 2 – Speak (Type) Your Response

Type what you would say directly to the patient:

Key Communication Points (What OEBC Expects)

  • Diagnosis: Name pterygium clearly; benign fibrovascular growth.
  • Etiology: UV exposure, wind, dust, outdoor work.
  • Current impact: Irritation, redness, cosmetic concern; vision normal.
  • Progression: May grow, induce astigmatism, impact future vision.
  • Non-surgical options: Lubrication, UV protection, environmental modifications.
  • Surgery indications: Vision threat, significant astigmatism, persistent inflammation, cosmetic concerns.
  • Recurrence risk: Possible even after surgery.
  • Follow-up: Typically annual, sooner with changes.
