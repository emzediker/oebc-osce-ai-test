OEBC Mock OSCE – Station 23

Ocular Disorders – Anterior Segment • Uveitis / Systemic Workup (Interactive)

Step 1 – Read the Station

Scenario:
A 32-year-old presents with a red, painful right eye for 3 days with photophobia and blurred vision. No trauma or CL wear. They had a similar episode 2 years ago.

Exam (OD):
VA: 20/40 → 20/25 PH
Ciliary flush
Cornea clear
AC: 2+ cells, 1+ flare
IOP: 18 OD / 15 OS
Pupil: miotic, sluggish
Fundus: normal

Your task: Explain the diagnosis and management in patient-friendly terms, including:

  • Diagnosis: anterior uveitis / iritis
  • Why the eye is red / painful
  • Steroid & cycloplegic drops and how to use them
  • Need for close follow-up & IOP monitoring
  • Possible systemic workup due to recurrence
  • Urgent symptoms that require reevaluation

Step 2 – Speak (Type) Your Response

Key Communication Points (What OEBC Expects)

  • Diagnosis: Clearly state anterior uveitis / iritis.
  • Mechanism: Inflammation inside the eye → redness, pain, photophobia.
  • Treatment:
    • Topical steroids (explain dosing & taper concept)
    • Cycloplegic (pain relief, prevents synechiae)
  • Monitoring: IOP checks + follow-up; do NOT stop steroids abruptly.
  • Systemic Workup: Possible autoimmune/infectious causes due to recurrence.
  • Red Flags: Worsening pain, vision loss, new floaters, flashes, severe headache.
  • Style: Simple, reassuring, patient-centered.
