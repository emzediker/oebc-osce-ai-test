OEBC Mock OSCE – Station 22

Ocular Disorders • Bacterial Keratitis (Interactive)

Step 1 – Read the Station

Scenario:
A 27-year-old soft contact lens wearer presents with severe pain, redness, photophobia, and decreased vision in the right eye. They admit to sleeping in their lenses and occasionally rinsing them with tap water. Slit lamp reveals a focal epithelial defect with a suppurative infiltrate and surrounding stromal edema.

Your task:
Explain to the patient what bacterial keratitis is, why it is serious, the role of contact lens hygiene, the need for urgent treatment, and the risks of delaying therapy. Provide clear, urgent but calm counselling.

You would have 8 minutes on the real OEBC OSCE.

Step 2 – Speak (Type) Your Response

Type what you would say to the patient:

Key Communication Points (What OEBC Expects)

  • State the diagnosis clearly: bacterial keratitis (corneal infection).
  • Explain the seriousness: risk of corneal scarring, vision loss, or perforation.
  • Link cause to risky lens use (sleeping in lenses, tap water) without shaming.
  • Stress that this is an ocular emergency.
  • Explain intensive antibiotic treatment + no contact lenses.
  • Emphasize strict follow-up and importance of rapid improvement.
  • Use calm but urgent communication.

Self-Assessment Rubric

Domain Criteria
Diagnosis Named bacterial keratitis clearly and accurately.
Explanation Explained the danger + symptoms in simple terms.
Management Discussed antibiotic therapy + no CL wear.
Prognosis & Follow-Up Urgency, risk of vision loss, and close monitoring.
Communication Clear, organized, non-judgmental, empathetic.
